Bidirectional Flow represents a corridor scenario in which two groups of pedestrians move in opposite directions within the same passage. Due to spontaneous lane formation, pedestrians moving in the same direction tend to align longitudinally, which increases following behavior along the walking direction, while lateral movement is mainly used to resolve conflicts with oncoming pedestrians. As a result, interactions exhibit a mixed pattern: longitudinal interactions are often cooperative and follower–leader-like, whereas lateral interactions are primarily avoidance-driven.
