Corner represents a unidirectional corridor scenario with a 90-degree turn, where pedestrians move along a bent passage and must change their walking direction sharply at the corner. Due to the geometric discontinuity and limited line of sight around the corner, pedestrians adjust their speed and trajectory in advance, especially under high-density conditions. Interactions are primarily driven by following behavior, speed adaptation, and local spacing regulation rather than head-on conflicts, with movement constraints dominated by corridor geometry.
