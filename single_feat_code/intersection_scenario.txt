Intersection represents a four-way crossing scenario where large numbers of pedestrians simultaneously enter the intersection from four orthogonal directions and move toward the opposite side. Pedestrian trajectories intersect within a shared central area, leading to frequent crossing conflicts, multidirectional and perpendicular interactions, and strong local collision-avoidance behavior. Movement is primarily goal-oriented toward the opposite exit, while trajectories are continuously adjusted through lateral deviations and speed modulation due to dense cross flows.
