ETH represents a real-world pedestrian trajectory dataset collected in outdoor public spaces. The dataset captures pedestrian movement in weakly constrained natural environments, such as open walkways and plazas, where no explicit entrances, exits, or corridor boundaries are imposed. Pedestrian density in ETH is relatively low, and interactions are typically sparse and short-lived, with most trajectories dominated by individual goal-directed movement rather than sustained collective dynamics. Compared to controlled experimental datasets, ETH exhibits high behavioral variability, heterogeneous walking directions, and limited emergence of crowd-level phenomena.
